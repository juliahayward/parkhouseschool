Introduction

Sleeping Dragons is a programme developed by the Clinical Psychologist and Speech and Language Therapist at Peterborough Child Development Unit (CDU), which provides specialist help for children with communication problems. It involves a multi-disciplinary team of experts working with the child, but critically it is a home-based approach in which parents are not only fully involved at every step, they are equipped through the programme so that as it progresses, they are able to provide the majority of interventions themselves.

The Sleeping Dragons Programme has assisted many children since April 1999. Most parents appear to find the programme useful and their children have developed skills in all the areas targeted. There has also been excellent attendance at the Sleeping Dragons meetings both from parents and from other people involved in working with the child.

The programme is currently limited to eight children at any one time. Participating children join the programme between the ages of 2 1/2 and 4 years, or when they begin nursery school. In addition to communication problems, the majority have an autistic spectrum disorder. However, the programme has also been designed to be used with children who have learning difficulties as well as communication problems. Meetings involving parents, the children and specialists are held every 8 weeks; these form the core of the programme and so it is very important that families are able to attend.
